  • Matplotlib
  • Pyinstaller

About

backCor is a pre-processing graphical tool designed for Raman spectra.
After being properly proecessed, Raman Spectra data is used to classify mammal microcalcification and therefore to detect breast tumor.
I designed and developed the software in collaboration with the Nanomedicine and Molecular Imaging lab (IRCCS ICS Maugeri) and the CCPS lab (UNIPV).
The project let me put into practice both my Computer engineering and Biomedical engineering skills I gained during my course of study and gave me the possibility to work in a real-world environment where communicating, discussing and getting continuous feedback from the labs members was crucial to deliver it.

COVID-19 Hackathon

  • Python
  • Numpy
  • Pandas
  • SciKit-learn
  • Matplotlib
  • Seaborn

About

The objective of the project/hackathon was to investigate COVID-19 data providing meaningful insights and trends.
As explained in the rules, the candidate needed to carry out a complete Data Science analysis that would have been evaluated based on originality, result correctness, presentation and complexity.
In my analysis I tried to assess the effectiveness of the Italian Governement containement measures.
The main idea I had was to visualize the number of new cases with respect to the total number of cases instead of doing that against time.
By doing this it was possible to demonstrate the effectiveness of the quarantine measures that otherwise would have been hidden by the exponential growth of the number of new cases.
The analysis was positively evaluated and got me the 2nd place in the COVID-19 Hackathon.

About

In this project, me and my group implemented and tested different server architectures and technologies to assess the performance and the robustness of the systems.
After setting up multiple machines on AWS running different protocols (HTTP/1.1, HTTP/2) and arranging them in simpler and more complex network architectures (Reverse proxies...), we defined a pipeline of tests to evaluate the performance in terms of Page Load Time, Round Trip Time and the overall stability.
To test that, we generated a big amount of HTTP traffic to the target configurations simulating a DDoS attack.
After doing that, we selected the best one for every single scenario.
For the project I focused on the HTTP/2 implementation, performance testing and in particular on exploiting the powerful Server Push feature that comes with it.

About

The goal of the project was to design a system that, given a image of a single six-sided die, was automatically able to determine the outcome of the roll (a number in the 1â€“6 range).
To do that, I designed a feature extraction procedure that extracts information from the images, I trained different classification models (Multinomial Logistic Regression, KSVM, MLP) and after analysing the behavior of the trained models I selected the best one for the purpose.
